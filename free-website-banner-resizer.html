✦ CSS Cropping vs. Pre-formatting

Developers often debate whether to format the image or use CSS (`object-fit: cover`) to fix the layout.

  • Relying on CSS: The browser guesses what part of the image to show. It often cuts off important text or the subject's face on mobile screens.
  • Pre-Formatting with SquarePic: You take full control. By padding the image into the exact banner shape beforehand, you guarantee that 100% of the image is always visible on every device.

✦ Frequently Asked Questions

What size should a website banner be?

For full-width hero banners, the standard recommended size is 1920 x 1080 pixels (16:9 ratio). For blog post headers and smaller banners, 1200 x 630 pixels is widely accepted and optimal for social sharing.

Will resizing make my banner blurry?

No. Our tool preserves the original resolution of your photo by padding the canvas instead of forcefully stretching pixels, guaranteeing your website looks sharp on retina and 4K displays.

Can I use this tool to make a banner from a square photo?
